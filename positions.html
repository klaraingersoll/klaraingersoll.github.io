Co-Founder & Co-Curator, The Laundry Room Gallery, U King's College
Spring 2017

Greta Hamilton and I received a grant from King's to renovate and create an art gallery and performance space out of a laundry room. The show featured works by students at King's and the Nova Scotia College of Art & Design and was centered around the theme of Hannah Arendt's notion of politics as we engaged in the process of making the formerly Private Realm (laundry room) Public (art gallery) and therefore Political: making what was formerly seen as Labor, now seen as Art. We held a public gallery opening for the larger community to come together and view the art and celebrate the gallery launch, featuring local bands.

Founder & Leader, Club 1, CRLS, Cambridge MA
January 2014 - June 2016

My high school, like most other high schools, was missing a safe and focused environment to discuss gender equity issues, so I founded the CRLS Feminist Coalition, "Club 1" in the winter of my sophomore year, heading and growing it until I graduated. Club 1 quickly became one of the most attended clubs at CRLS. I led weekly workshops on the key building blocks of feminist analysis. After our group became an educated constituency, we took on our first issue, which was the school's antiquated dress code, disproportionately targeting women of color. After a successful campaign of educating the administrators and faculty, the dress code was rewritten.

After 'addressing the dress code,' we decided to take on a more challenging issue: sexual harassment and assault on campus, and the culture of victim blaming surrounding them. Because this issue was hidden, we wanted to both learn about it and create social awareness around it, so we spoke extensively with survivors, conducted online surveys, and held public forums for anyone to share experiences with sexual violence in the CRLS community. To pressure the administration to actually implement change, and properly enforce Title IX, I led a highly successful Walkout accompanied by a letter listing our demands, signed by hundreds of Walkout participants, which we presented to the administration. The Walkout garnered media attention and caused the School Committee to request progress reports from the school administration on implementing change. Since, I met frequently with the school principal, and the school continues to make progress rewriting their policy and protocol.
